About Indian Agriculture

Agriculture in India is diverse — from irrigated rice paddies in the Gangetic plains to rainfed millet fields in the Deccan and plantation crops in the hills. Climatic zones and soils determine what grows best where. Krishi.AI helps you match soil, crop, and fertilizer guidance to local conditions.

Seasons & Cropping

Kharif (monsoon) • Rabi (winter) • Zaid (summer). Crop selection depends on rainfall, temperature and soil type.

Soil Health

Maintain organic matter, prevent erosion, and use balanced N-P-K based on a soil test. Micro-nutrients and pH matter.

Sustainability

Crop rotation, intercropping, composting and integrated pest management (IPM) reduce inputs and increase resilience.

Irrigation & Water

Use water-efficient practices — drip irrigation, mulching and scheduling to crop needs reduce waste.

Quick Tips for Farmers

  • Always use locally-recommended seed varieties.
  • Get a soil test before applying fertilizers.
  • Adopt multi-year crop rotation to reduce pests and improve soil.
  • Use organic matter (compost, green manures) to improve structure.
